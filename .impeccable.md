## Design Context

### Users
**Primary**: Recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Allen Saji for engineering roles. They arrive from a CV link or LinkedIn, spend 60–120 seconds skimming, and decide whether to reach out. They need to quickly read technical credibility and get a sense of who Allen is as a person — not just a list of skills.

**Secondary**: Fellow engineers and collaborators who want to go deeper — read the project case studies, check the GitHub links, understand the technical decisions behind the work.

**Job to be done**: Impress fast, then reward curiosity. The surface layer communicates competence; the interactive details reward anyone who sticks around.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Chaotic, fun, professional.

Unpacked: Allen is technically serious (final-year engineering student spanning software and hardware, honours research, real distributed systems projects) but refuses to be boring about it. The "chaotic" and "fun" don't mean sloppy — they mean the site has a point of view and a personality that bleeds through. Think: controlled chaos. Unexpected but never incoherent.

**Emotional goal**: Visitors should feel a mix of *impressed* (by the technical depth) and *charmed* (by the personality). The ideal response is "this person clearly knows what they're doing AND seems like they'd be fun to work with."

### Aesthetic Direction
**Core aesthetic**: Retro-futurism. Warm paper light mode / neon-glow dark mode. Hard offset drop shadows. Monospace type mixed with humanist sans. The aesthetic has a strong identity — lean into it, don't water it down.

**Dark mode** is the primary experience (default on load). The neon-glow dark palette (#0F0A1E base, yellow/pink/orange accents) is the stronger aesthetic statement and what most visitors will see.

**Motion**: Smooth and fluid, never flashy. Animations should feel earned — stagger reveals, panel transitions, the typewriter name effect. Nothing gratuitous. Allen explicitly wants interactions to feel polished without being showy.

**Anti-references**: Generic Tailwind templates. Portfolios that look like every other portfolio. Anything that feels like it came from a starter kit. The whole point is that this site is hand-built and feels like it.

**Responsive**: Both desktop and mobile matter equally. Desktop has the sophisticated hover-preview → click-to-expand timeline interaction. Mobile has an independent tap-to-toggle model. Neither is an afterthought.

### Design Principles
1. **Controlled chaos over sterile precision** — the site should have unexpected moments and strong personality, but never lose its professional footing. Quirkiness that serves the brand, not randomness.
2. **Technical depth on demand** — don't front-load every detail. The timeline interaction model (skim cards → hover preview → click to expand) is the right mental model for the whole site.
3. **Retro-futurism as identity, not decoration** — the offset shadows, warm palette, and monospace type aren't stylistic flourishes; they're the signature. Every design decision should reinforce or complement this direction.
4. **Fluid and polished, never showy** — animations are purposeful state communication, not performance. If an animation doesn't help a visitor understand something, it doesn't belong.
5. **Both screens are first class** — mobile visitors (often recruiters on phones) get a complete, well-considered experience, not a shrunken desktop layout.
